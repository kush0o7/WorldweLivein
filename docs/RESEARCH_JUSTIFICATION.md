# Research Justification

This model uses a small set of economic and geopolitical research frameworks. Each is chosen because it:
1) has a clear empirical basis, and  
2) can be translated into interpretable signals.

---

## 1. Reinhart‑Rogoff Debt Thresholds
**Source:** “This Time Is Different” (2009)  
**Why included:** debt levels correlate with lower growth and crisis probability.  
**How used:** debt ratios raise crisis risk and apply a GDP penalty.

---

## 2. Acemoglu‑Restrepo Automation Displacement
**Source:** “Robots and Jobs” (2018/2022), “The Wrong Kind of AI?” (2023)  
**Why included:** automation changes labor share, wages, and inequality.  
**How used:** AI investment and layoffs proxy automation intensity, which feeds labor share and Gini.

---

## 3. Nordhaus DICE Climate Damage
**Source:** DICE model (2017)  
**Why included:** climate has measurable GDP drag.  
**How used:** damage = α × T² with non‑linear tipping above 2°C.

---

## 4. Richardson / Correlates of War
**Source:** Richardson (1960), COW Project  
**Why included:** arms races and alliance polarization increase war risk.  
**How used:** geopolitical tension + war count feed war probability and conflict acceleration.

---

## 5. Brynjolfsson J‑Curve (GPT Lag)
**Source:** Brynjolfsson, Rock & Syverson (2021)  
**Why included:** GPTs depress measured productivity before a surge.  
**How used:** AI adoption sets phase (investment / reorg / harvest).

---

## 6. Dalio Long‑Term Debt Cycle
**Source:** Dalio (2018, 2021)  
**Why included:** macro cycles have predictable stress phases.  
**How used:** debt + inequality + currency trend → cycle risk.

---

## 7. Market Signals Layer (Yield Curve + Credit Spreads)
**Source:** empirical recession indicators  
**Why included:** market signals often lead macro data.  
**How used:** yield curve inversion and widening spreads increase stress drag.

---

## 8. Policy Reaction Function
**Source:** Taylor Rule (1993)  
**Why included:** policy rates drive GDP impulse.  
**How used:** compares Taylor‑implied rate vs actual policy rate and computes drag.

---

## Why These Models Together
No single model captures the whole world. The model is **hybrid**:
- Historical analogies for narrative structure  
- Research models for measurable mechanisms  
- Monte Carlo for tails and regime shifts
